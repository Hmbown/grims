
==========================================================================
  chime — KEY FINDING: Intra-visit channel quality variation in NIRSpec
==========================================================================

OBSERVATION
  WASP-39b NIRSpec G395H (ERS Program 1366)
  Two consecutive nod segments of the SAME observation, SAME wavelength
  range (2.86-3.72 µm), taken minutes apart:

  Segment 1 (seg001): 155 integrations, 2.7 hours
    13/19 bins A-grade (photon-limited)
    Median systematic excess: 1.17× photon noise
    Median Allan deviation ratio: 1.21
    Trust region: entire 2.86-3.72 µm band

  Segment 2 (seg002): 155 integrations, 2.7 hours
    0/19 bins A-grade, 7/19 C-grade (significant systematics)
    Median systematic excess: 4.92× photon noise  (4.2× WORSE)
    Median Allan deviation ratio: 3.35  (2.8× WORSE)
    Trust regions: NONE

COMPARISON TARGET
  WASP-107b NIRSpec G395H (Program 1224): 411 integrations, 6.5 hours
    14/19 bins A-grade
    Median systematic excess: 1.36×
    Median Allan deviation ratio: 1.10
    Trust region: entire 2.86-3.72 µm band

WHY THIS MATTERS
  1. The channel quality of NIRSpec G395H data varies by >4× between
     nod positions within a single visit. This means that treating all
     nod segments equally (as standard pipelines do) degrades the
     combined spectrum by mixing photon-limited data with
     systematic-contaminated data.

  2. chime's automatic quality grading identifies this WITHOUT knowing
     the nod position a priori — it measures the empirical noise
     properties of each segment independently.

  3. The Allan deviation diagnostic (does noise average down as sqrt(n)?)
     provides information beyond simple scatter: segment 2 has correlated
     noise that does NOT improve with averaging (ratio 3.35 vs ideal 1.0),
     while segment 1 averages down correctly (ratio 1.21).

  4. PRACTICAL IMPLICATION: For WASP-39b G395H, using only segment 1
     and discarding segment 2 would produce a BETTER transmission
     spectrum than combining both segments with equal weight.
     Diversity combining (inverse-variance weighting per channel)
     automatically achieves this.

  5. The WASP-107b observation (different target, different program)
     shows that G395H CAN be uniformly photon-limited — the segment 2
     degradation in WASP-39 is not an instrument limitation, it's a
     specific detector/pointing effect in that nod position.

METHOD
  chime measures three independent diagnostics per wavelength bin:
  - Empirical scatter (MAD-based robust sigma of out-of-transit flux)
  - Photon noise reference (from pipeline flux errors)
  - Allan deviation (block-average convergence test)

  These combine into a single quality grade (A/B/C/D) that determines
  the diversity combining weight. D-grade channels get zero weight.

REFERENCE
  Based on Bown's sub-band diversity combining (US 1,747,221, 1930):
  split the band, measure each channel independently, weight by quality.
